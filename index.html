The First Institute of Human Potential Realization

Existing sciences study the human being in fragments — medicine studies the body, psychology the mind, sociology the context. No discipline has yet asked the central question: why does a person not realize their full potential — and how can this be measured and changed?

Scientific Departments

Seven disciplines.
One object of study.

01
Anthroponomy
The foundational science of FIOHPR. Studies what human potential is, how to measure it, and why people fail to realize it. The physics of the human being.
02
Symmetrology
Studies the symmetry of the human system, functional imbalances, morphological and functional asymmetry, and the laws of human system harmonization.
03
Human Potential Measurement
The science of measuring potential. Includes Potential Index, Potential Gap, Growth Velocity, and the Human Coordinates System.
04
Human Systems Science
Studies the human as an integrated system: body, nervous system, cognitive functions, emotions, behavior, environment, and life trajectory.
05
Human Alignment Science
Studies internal coherence, goal conflict, function alignment, and system stability. The science of the Human Alignment state.
06
Human Navigation
Studies the current and potential state, development routes, risks, scenarios, and the strategy of potential realization.
07
Human Potential Observatory
Research department. Observes global data, development trends, constraint factors, human potential maps, and forecasts.
